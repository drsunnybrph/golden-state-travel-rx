Prescription Furnishing — How It Works

Under California Business & Professions Code § 4052(a)(10)(A)(3) and 16 CCR § 1746.5, a trained California pharmacist may independently furnish CDC-recommended travel medications that don't require a diagnosis — no physician visit and no collaborative practice agreement. After your consultation, eligible medications are transmitted electronically to the pharmacy you choose (CVS, Walgreens, Costco, or your local independent) for convenient pickup.

  • Malaria prophylaxis (atovaquone-proguanil, doxycycline, mefloquine)
  • Traveler's diarrhea standby antibiotics (azithromycin, rifaximin)
  • Altitude sickness prophylaxis (acetazolamide)
  • Motion sickness (scopolamine patch)
  • Vaccine schedule planning & clinic referral (hepatitis A, typhoid, yellow fever)

Eligibility is determined during your consultation through a good-faith clinical evaluation and travel history, per 16 CCR § 1746.5. Your primary care provider is notified within 14 days, or you receive a written record if you have none.
